From the beginning of the 1940s, Japan's global position was at a disadvantage, and it no longer had the strength to face Korea's fierce resistance. The resistance fighters, who did not miss this momentum, spurred the liberation by carrying out more active military and political maneuvers. They devised a way to record Bluebird's sound and spread it nationwide. People can hear it through the gramophone. In addition, they produced a mobile terminal that can send and receive Arirang codes. No more stamps needed. Long live Korea's independence! Upon hearing the news of liberation, the Japanese police who imprisoned and tortured the Resistance were busy running away. Many people still have birds in their hearts. These days it seems to call the bird by a different name: Twitter
